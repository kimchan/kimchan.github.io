Welcome!



I’m Kim and I’m a Senior UX Designer.
The core of my UX belief is research driven design, so that by listening to the user we can create valuable products for them.

I’m a Senior product designer, creating intuitive wireframes and prototypes and usability testing with our users.

I’m a researcher, learning and caring about understanding our users’ world and their behaviors.

I’m a collaborator, communicating and building strong relationships to move the product forward.

I’m a full stack Senior software engineer, coding and pairing in an agile environment.

I’m a facilitator, leading productive meetings and exercises where everyone has a voice.

I’m a strategist, thinking outside the box, advocating for the user, and determining business value.

Born and raised in sunny Florida, my career in technology and UX started at the University of Florida.

Originally a microbiology major due to being a science geek, I took one Java course which introduced me into a new world of software development and felt immediately connected to the challenge of coding and developing working applications that I could concoct from my imagination.

I switched my major to Digital Arts and Sciences which was a combination of my interests in engineering and design. At school I learned technical skills in computer science with a focus on human centered computing.

During my internships, I exercised my proficiency in coding and experienced software product development lifecycles from a startup environment to working at a Fortune 50 company. I graduated with my Bachelors in the College of Engineering in 2013.

I started my career as a software engineer at The Home Depot in Atlanta. I worked in an agile environment pairing and collaborating on building software for HR applications.

I joined the intern committee at THD and help shape their summer intern experience. I interview and recruit talented students and become a mentor to them in the program.

Although engineering was fun, I wanted to have more influence on the direction of the product, so I transitioned into a Senior UX designer role where I get to flex my creativity muscles. For me UX is the perfect concentration of all my strengths. Not only do I get to design, but I get to talk to our customers, understand their processes and pain points, and empathize with them.

I love being able to help solve problems by strategizing and designing software that makes their life easier.

Now that you know a little about me, I'd like to learn about you!

Want to share camping tips? Chat about places you've explored? Swap recipes? Discuss UX strategies?

Please email me at kim.s.chan@gmail.com. I'd love to meet you!
